Building Partnerships: Collaborating for Greater Impact

At LES AMIS SOLIDAIRES, we believe that the most significant social change happens when organizations work together. Our strategic partnerships with hospitals, community centers, government agencies, and corporate sponsors have amplified our impact across Canada, allowing us to serve more people more effectively than ever before.

The Power of Collaboration

In 2024, we formalized partnerships with 45 organizations across Canada, creating a network of support that extends far beyond what any single organization could achieve alone. These partnerships have not only expanded our reach but also enhanced the quality and comprehensiveness of our services.

Government Collaboration: Immigrant Integration

Our partnerships with federal and provincial immigration agencies have created seamless pathways for newcomer support:

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)

Official settlement agency designation, funding for language training programs, and direct referral system for newly arrived refugees and immigrants.

Ontario Ministry of Immigration

Bridge training programs for internationally educated professionals and employment mentorship initiatives.

BC Provincial Nominee Program

Pre-arrival services, community orientation programs, and employer engagement initiatives.

Innovation Through Partnership: The Digital Bridge Project

One of our most successful partnership initiatives is the Digital Bridge Project, developed in collaboration with tech companies and educational institutions:

Project Partners:
  • University of Toronto: Curriculum development and student volunteers
  • Microsoft Canada: Software licenses and technical training
  • Toronto Public Library: Computer lab access and digital literacy resources
  • Seneca College: Instructor training and certification programs

This project has helped over 300 immigrants develop essential digital skills, with 78% finding employment in tech-related fields within six months of completion.
